How this works

Money you spend has already been taxed once, and it could have been invested instead. This calculator restates a single expense in three ways so the real trade-off is easy to see.

What you pay is the cash that actually leaves your account. For a recurring cost it is the full amount added up across your whole horizon.

What you earn (pre-tax) grosses the spend back up to the income it took to fund it. At a 40% marginal rate, every £60 spent needed £100 of salary to begin with.

What you give up is the headline figure. It is what that money would be worth if you invested it instead and left it to compound for your chosen horizon.

The two routes

The ISA route invests the post-tax cash you would otherwise have spent. It grows tax-free and comes out tax-free, so the future value is simply your money compounded.

The pension route invests the gross income behind the spend, because that money could have gone into a pension and picked up tax relief on the way in. The pot grows tax-free, but withdrawals are taxed: 25% comes out tax-free and the rest is taxed at your retirement rate. The pension route usually ends up larger, because relief puts more money to work from day one and a quarter of it escapes tax entirely.

Setting the return

Enter a real (after-inflation) return so every figure reads in today's money. Around 5% is a common long-run assumption for global equities. Lower it for a more cautious view, or raise it to stress-test the upside.

Reading the chart

The blue line is what you have handed over so far. The purple line is what the same money would be worth invested. The shaded wedge between them is the cost of spending rather than investing, and it widens the longer your horizon. Hover or drag along it to read the gap in any year.
