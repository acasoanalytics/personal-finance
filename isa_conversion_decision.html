Loss-aversion weight λ
λ is how much more a realised loss hurts than an equal gain feels good. λ = 1 weighs them the same (risk-neutral — the loss-aversion view then matches expected value). Most people sit around 1.5–2.5: at λ = 2, losing £100 stings about twice as much as gaining £100 pleases. It's applied only to the left corner here, because that's the one where you actually sell at a loss.

These don't change which strategy wins on average — they're the size and odds of the regret in each strategy's weak corner. The felt figures apply the loss-aversion weight λ to the left corner only, because it's a realised, forced loss; the right corner is a missed gain, so it isn't amplified. A small cash buffer only earns its keep against the left one.
